Journalism Introduction

Problem Statement

The definition of what journalism is, who journalists are and how to best use the dazzling array of tools now available to reach a global audience are all questions at the heart of this app. The demand for quality journalism in the Middle East and North Africa has perhaps never been greater. People need access to information that is accurate, timely and relevant to their lives.

The challenge for citizen journalists and established news organizations alike is to work together to collect and distribute this important information. Essential to this process is the development of better communication and a set of standards that works for everyone. How do citizen journalists collect and report information that established outlets can use? In turn, how do established news organizations better support dedicated citizen journalists and take advantage of what they have to offer?

Background

Throughout the 20th Century the costs of publishing newspapers and magazines or broadcasting on TV or radio was so great that the news was controlled by a relatively small number of professional outlets. These organizations can still bring powerful resources to bear in their coverage and maintained systems of checks and balances within their institutions to eliminate -- or at least minimize -- personal bias and the publication of inaccurate information.

These outlets are built on large networks of highly trained reporters, most of whom learned on the job by working in newsrooms with more experienced journalists. In some countries journalists are licensed or belong to professional associations. At its best, this system of journalism maintains the highest standards of accuracy and can take on big, important issues. At its worst it can be indifferent or corrupt -- a mouthpiece for government or other powerful interest groups.

The Internet has changed the landscape because traditional news organizations no longer have sole control of the ability to distribute information to large audiences. At the same time, smartphones and other tools have come into the hands of large numbers people with important stories to tell. These citizen journalists often represent communities and issues overlooked by traditional media or, just as often, communities that traditional outlets could not access. In some cases citizen journalists report on issues that government and other groups don’t want covered.

The challenge for citizen journalists today is to produce material that meets the highest professional standards. All journalists are trained observers. We are passionate about the work we do, but we also have to be highly professional in how we tell our stories. This app will provide guidelines for how to do just that.

Solution

It doesn’t matter if you’re an independent citizen journalist with a smartphone or a highly trained professional working for Al Jazeera or the BBC, the fundamentals of good journalism are the same: your coverage needs to be accurate, it needs to be balanced, it needs to be fair, and you need to be transparent about the process by which you collected information.

Your coverage should also be fact-based. What does that mean? As noted above, journalism is evolving. The shape of journalism in the future will surely differ globally from region to region and even from country to country. Still, there are some guiding principles. Fact-based journalism is a style of journalism that bases coverage on facts rather than opinion or even personal belief. It values things you can prove over personal views or a publisher’s agenda. It values things you can demonstrate over speculation.

The journalism module and the Story Maker app more broadly will walk you through the key skills you need to develop good fact-based techniques: how to report well; writing basics; identifying sources; interview techniques; the different types of news coverage and how to talk to large news networks about your work.

When to Use / When Not to

In theory there is never a reason not to employ the fundamentals of good, fact-based journalism. But, the real-life realities and working conditions many citizen journalists face can make it hard. Personal safety, both physical and digital, can be an impediment to news coverage. Access to good sources with good information is sometimes very difficult if not impossible.

The skills covered in this module and throughout the app will help you better understand and manage these challenging realities.
